## Claim 1. Hybrid origin of Cx. pallens from Cx. pipiens x Cx. quinquefasciatus

### Fonseca, Smith, Kim & Mogi 2009 — HIGH
- **Authors:** Fonseca DM, Smith JL, Kim H-C, Mogi M.
- **Year / Journal:** 2009. *Infection, Genetics and Evolution* 9(6): 1197-1203.
- **DOI:** 10.1016/j.meegid.2009.06.023
- **PubMed/PMCID:** PMC2787783
- **Title:** Population genetics of the mosquito *Culex pipiens pallens* reveals sex-linked asymmetric introgression by *Culex quinquefasciatus*.
- **What it says:** Used ACE-2 intron sequencing and eight microsatellite loci in Japan, Korea, and China. Found that *Cx. p. pallens* has a unique microsatellite signature distinct from European *Cx. p. pipiens*, but every male *Cx. p. pallens* sampled carried both "pallens" and "quinquefasciatus" ACE bands, while females overwhelmingly carried only "pallens." Concluded asymmetric, sex-linked introgression from *Cx. quinquefasciatus* into *Cx. p. pallens*. Explicitly tests and supports the hypothesis that *Cx. p. pallens* arose via hybridization between *Cx. pipiens* and *Cx. quinquefasciatus* but is now its own distinct lineage.
- **Useful quote:** "asymmetric introgression of *Cx. quinquefasciatus* genetic material across chromosomal regions into *Cx. p. pallens*"

### Aardema, vonHoldt, Fritz & Davis 2020 — HIGH
- **Authors:** Aardema ML, vonHoldt BM, Fritz ML, Davis SR.
- **Year / Journal:** 2020. *Parasites & Vectors* 13(1): 8.
- **DOI:** 10.1186/s13071-019-3879-8 (PubMed: 31915057; PMCID: PMC6950815)
- **Title:** Global evaluation of taxonomic relationships and admixture within the *Culex pipiens* complex of mosquitoes.
- **What it says:** Genome-wide RADseq across the six recognized *Cx. pipiens* complex taxa. Reports that *Cx. p. pallens* samples carry ~70% *Cx. quinquefasciatus* and ~30% *Cx. pipiens*/*molestus* ancestry, plus a substantial complement of unique variants, consistent with a hybrid origin followed by independent evolution.
- **Useful quote:** "may have arisen from past hybridization" between *Cx. quinquefasciatus* and *Cx. pipiens*.

### Cornel, McAbee, Rasgon, Stanich, Scott & Coetzee 2003 — HIGH (cited in Fonseca 2009 reference list verbatim)
- **Authors:** Cornel AJ, McAbee RD, Rasgon J, Stanich MA, Scott TW, Coetzee M.
- **Year / Journal:** 2003. *Journal of Medical Entomology* 40(1): 36-51.
- **DOI:** 10.1603/0022-2585-40.1.36 (PubMed: 12597651)
- **Title:** Differences in extent of genetic introgression between sympatric *Culex pipiens* and *Culex quinquefasciatus* (Diptera: Culicidae) in California and South Africa.
- **What it says:** Documents extensive bidirectional introgression where *Cx. pipiens* and *Cx. quinquefasciatus* meet in sympatry, using allozymes and phallosome morphology. Provides the New-World analogue of the East-Asian *pallens* situation and is widely cited as evidence that these two taxa hybridize freely in nature.

### Kothera, Zimmerman, Richards & Savage 2009 — HIGH
- **Authors:** Kothera L, Zimmerman EM, Richards CM, Savage HM.
- **Year / Journal:** 2009. *Journal of Medical Entomology* 46(2): 236-248.
- **DOI:** 10.1603/033.046.0208 (PubMed: 19351074)
- **Title:** Microsatellite characterization of subspecies and their hybrids in *Culex pipiens* complex (Diptera: Culicidae) mosquitoes along a north-south transect in the central United States.
- **What it says:** Microsatellite evidence that *Cx. p. pipiens* and *Cx. p. quinquefasciatus* hybridize extensively where their ranges meet (e.g., Memphis, TN). Supports the broader claim that interfertile *pipiens/quinquefasciatus* hybridization is recurrent and geographically widespread.

### Supplementary supporting references (HIGH)
- **Smith JL & Fonseca DM. 2004.** Rapid assays for identification of members of the *Culex (Culex) pipiens* complex, their hybrids, and other sibling species. *Am J Trop Med Hyg* 70(4): 339-345. — Establishes the ACE-2 diagnostic that flags hybridization in the complex.

## Claim 2. Cx. pipiens complex composition and the biology of Cx. molestus as the autogenous urban form

### Vinogradova 2000 — HIGH (book)
- **Author:** Vinogradova EB.
- **Year / Publisher:** 2000. Pensoft Publishers, Sofia-Moscow. ISBN 954-642-104-3. (Pensoft Series Parasitologica No. 2.)
- **Title:** *Culex pipiens pipiens* mosquitoes: taxonomy, distribution, ecology, physiology, genetics, applied importance and control.
- **What it says:** Monograph-scale review of complex taxonomy, the *pipiens* vs *molestus* biotype distinction, urban underground habitat and autogeny of *molestus*, distribution, vector competence, and control. The canonical reference for complex biology.

### Vinogradova 2003 — HIGH
- **Author:** Vinogradova EB.
- **Year / Journal:** 2003. *Acta Societatis Zoologicae Bohemicae* 67: 41-50.
- **Title:** Ecophysiological and morphological variations in mosquitoes of the *Culex pipiens* complex (Diptera: Culicidae).
- **What it says:** Distills the morphological indistinguishability of complex members and documents the ecophysiological differences (autogeny, stenogamy, hypogean habitat, lack of diapause) that define *molestus* relative to nominate *pipiens*.

### Farajollahi, Fonseca, Kramer & Kilpatrick 2011 — HIGH
- **Authors:** Farajollahi A, Fonseca DM, Kramer LD, Kilpatrick AM.
- **Year / Journal:** 2011. *Infection, Genetics and Evolution* 11(7): 1577-1585.
- **DOI:** 10.1016/j.meegid.2011.08.013 (PubMed: 21875691; PMCID: PMC3190018)
- **Title:** "Bird biting" mosquitoes and human disease: a review of the role of *Culex pipiens* complex mosquitoes in epidemiology.
- **What it says:** Standard review enumerating complex members (*Cx. p. pipiens* with pipiens/molestus forms, *Cx. p. pallens*, *Cx. quinquefasciatus*, *Cx. australicus*, *Cx. globocoxitus*) and reviewing their role in WNV, SLEV, avian malaria, and filariasis transmission.

### Fonseca, Keyghobadi, Malcolm, Mehmet, Schaffner, Mogi, Fleischer & Wilkerson 2004 — HIGH
- **Authors:** Fonseca DM, Keyghobadi N, Malcolm CA, Mehmet C, Schaffner F, Mogi M, Fleischer RC, Wilkerson RC.
- **Year / Journal:** 2004. *Science* 303(5663): 1535-1538.
- **DOI:** 10.1126/science.1094247 (PubMed: 15001783)
- **Title:** Emerging vectors in the *Culex pipiens* complex.
- **What it says:** Microsatellite evidence that European *pipiens* and *molestus* are reproductively isolated above-ground/below-ground forms, but North American populations show ubiquitous hybridization — directly relevant to complex composition and bridge-vector hypothesis for WNV.

### Bahnck & Fonseca 2006 — HIGH (supplementary)
- **Authors:** Bahnck CM, Fonseca DM. 2006. *Am J Trop Med Hyg* 75(2): 251-255. PubMed: 16896127.
- Rapid CQ11 assay distinguishing *pipiens* form *pipiens* from *molestus* and hybrids.

## Claim 3. Phylogenetic discordance (low gCF/sCF) as signature of introgression or hybrid origin, not just ILS

### Minh, Hahn & Lanfear 2020 — HIGH (the gCF/sCF paper itself)
- **Authors:** Minh BQ, Hahn MW, Lanfear R.
- **Year / Journal:** 2020. *Molecular Biology and Evolution* 37(9): 2727-2733.
- **DOI:** 10.1093/molbev/msaa106 (PubMed: 32365179; PMCID: PMC7475031)
- **Title:** New methods to calculate concordance factors for phylogenomic datasets.
- **What it says:** Introduces gene concordance factor (gCF) and site concordance factor (sCF) implemented in IQ-TREE as complements to bootstrap; explicitly framed as a way to "describe underlying disagreement among loci and sites" so that discordance from ILS vs. introgression vs. error can be examined. The canonical method citation.

### Hibbins & Hahn 2022 — HIGH
- **Authors:** Hibbins MS, Hahn MW.
- **Year / Journal:** 2022. *Genetics* 220(2): iyab173.
- **DOI:** 10.1093/genetics/iyab173 (PubMed: 34788444; PMCID: PMC9208645)
- **Title:** Phylogenomic approaches to detecting and characterizing introgression.
- **What it says:** Comprehensive review of how to distinguish ILS from introgression using genome-wide data — D-statistic, DFOIL, QuIBL, phylogenetic networks, and tests like D1 (Hibbins & Hahn 2019) that explicitly distinguish hybrid speciation from post-speciation introgression. Directly supports the argument that asymmetric/heightened gene-tree discordance is a hybridization signature.

### Degnan & Rosenberg 2009 — HIGH
- **Authors:** Degnan JH, Rosenberg NA.
- **Year / Journal:** 2009. *Trends in Ecology & Evolution* 24(6): 332-340.
- **DOI:** 10.1016/j.tree.2009.01.009
- **Title:** Gene tree discordance, phylogenetic inference and the multispecies coalescent.
- **What it says:** Foundational review establishing the multispecies coalescent expectation for gene-tree discordance under pure ILS — necessary to argue that observed discordance exceeds the ILS-only null and therefore implicates an additional process such as introgression.

### NOT VERIFIED in this session
- A specific Folk & Soltis 2018 paper on concordance/introgression in angiosperms was searched for but I could not pin down a matching reference. Recommend instead citing Folk, Soltis & Soltis 2018 (*Am J Bot* 105: 291-307, "A framework for sequence-based delimitation of incipient species") or Folk et al. 2017 (*PNAS* on Heuchera introgression) only after the authors verify the exact match — **omitted here**.

## Claim 4. Cx. pipiens complex as West Nile, lymphatic filariasis, and arbovirus vectors

### Hayes, Komar, Nasci, Montgomery, O'Leary & Campbell 2005 — HIGH
- **Authors:** Hayes EB, Komar N, Nasci RS, Montgomery SP, O'Leary DR, Campbell GL.
- **Year / Journal:** 2005. *Emerging Infectious Diseases* 11(8): 1167-1173.
- **DOI:** 10.3201/eid1108.050289a (PubMed: 16102302)
- **Title:** Epidemiology and transmission dynamics of West Nile virus disease.
- **What it says:** Reviews vector roles, with *Cx. pipiens* as the dominant northern US enzootic/bridge vector and *Cx. quinquefasciatus* dominant in the South; documents the 2002 outbreak and role of complex species in maintenance/amplification.

### Farajollahi et al. 2011 — HIGH (already cited in Claim 2)
- Reviews complex role in WNV, SLEV, avian malaria, and lymphatic filariasis. Doubles as a primary vector-biology citation.

### Bartholomay, Waterhouse et al. 2010 — HIGH (additional)
- **Authors:** Bartholomay LC, Waterhouse RM, Mayhew GF, et al. 2010. *Science* 330(6000): 88-90. DOI: 10.1126/science.1193162. PubMed: 20929811.
- Pathogenomics of *Cx. quinquefasciatus* — meta-analysis of vector–pathogen interactions across arboviruses, filaria, bacteria, malaria. Useful for medical-entomology framing of the complex.

### Arensburger, Megy, Waterhouse et al. 2010 — HIGH (additional)
- 2010. *Science* 330(6000): 86-88. DOI: 10.1126/science.1191864. PubMed: 20929810. "Sequencing of *Culex quinquefasciatus* establishes a platform for mosquito comparative genomics." Primary *Cx. quinquefasciatus* reference genome.

## Claim 5. Pangenome concept applied to eukaryotes / mosquitoes / insects

### Tettelin, Masignani, Cieslewicz et al. 2005 — HIGH
- **Authors:** Tettelin H, Masignani V, Cieslewicz MJ, et al.
- **Year / Journal:** 2005. *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences USA* 102(39): 13950-13955.
- **DOI:** 10.1073/pnas.0506758102
- **Title:** Genome analysis of multiple pathogenic isolates of *Streptococcus agalactiae*: implications for the microbial "pan-genome."
- **What it says:** The paper that coined "pan-genome" and partitioned it into core vs. dispensable components. Canonical origin citation for the concept.

### Bayer, Golicz, Scheben, Batley & Edwards 2020 — HIGH
- **Authors:** Bayer PE, Golicz AA, Scheben A, Batley J, Edwards D.
- **Year / Journal:** 2020. *Nature Plants* 6(8): 914-920.
- **DOI:** 10.1038/s41477-020-0733-0
- **Title:** Plant pan-genomes are the new reference.
- **What it says:** Reviews extension of the pangenome concept from microbes to eukaryotes (plants), motivating why a single reference genome underestimates intraspecific diversity. Good "concept transfer to eukaryotes" citation.

### Neafsey, Waterhouse, Abai et al. 2015 — HIGH
- **Authors:** Neafsey DE, Waterhouse RM, Abai MR, et al.
- **Year / Journal:** 2015. *Science* 347(6217): 1258522.
- **DOI:** 10.1126/science.1258522 (PubMed: 25554792)
- **Title:** Highly evolvable malaria vectors: the genomes of 16 *Anopheles* mosquitoes.
- **What it says:** Comparative genomics across 16 *Anopheles* species; documents elevated gene gain/loss, X-chromosome rearrangement, and intron turnover compared with *Drosophila*. The standard prior-art citation for mosquito-clade comparative/pangenomic analysis.

### NOT VERIFIED in this session
- A formal *Aedes aegypti* "pangenome" paper analogous to the *Anopheles* multi-genome project was searched for but I did not find a single canonical pangenome paper for *Aedes*. Recent linked-read inversion work (Redmond et al. 2020, *PLoS Genet*) and exon-enriched diversity papers exist but are not strictly pangenome studies. Recommend the manuscript state pangenome work on *Aedes* is limited rather than cite a possibly non-existent paper.

## Claims with weak / missing reference support — recommend softening or rephrasing

1. **An *Aedes aegypti* pangenome paper.** No single canonical *Aedes* pangenome study was located. Suggest phrasing as "Comparable pangenome-scale resources for *Aedes aegypti* and *Anopheles* species complexes remain limited; the most extensive multi-genome comparative work is Neafsey et al. 2015 for *Anopheles*" rather than implying one exists.
2. **A Mukha mtDNA / Far-East Russia *pallens* hybrid paper.** A relevant Mukha et al. 2005 *Russian Journal of Genetics* paper on mtDNA polymorphism and *Wolbachia* in Russian *Cx. pipiens* exists (PubMed 15865286) but it does not directly demonstrate *pallens* hybrid origin; it is a Wolbachia/mtDNA survey. Do not use it to support the hybrid-origin claim; the Fonseca 2009 and Aardema 2020 papers carry the load.
3. **Folk & Soltis 2018 ILS vs introgression citation.** Not located in this session as a discrete reference. Hibbins & Hahn 2022 and Degnan & Rosenberg 2009 together cover the methodological framing adequately; recommend omitting Folk & Soltis unless an author has the exact citation on hand.
4. **A Sasa / Kobayashi original crossing-experiment paper on *pallens* hybrid origin.** Sasa et al. 1967 (PubMed 5297295, "Crossing experiments between fatigans, pallens and molestus colonies of the mosquito *Culex pipiens* s.l. from Japan and Southern Asia, with special reference to hatchability of hybrid eggs") exists and is the historical record of the experimental crosses; it is **HIGH** confidence as a real paper but its abstract is in Japanese and I did not verify the precise journal/volume in this session. Worth confirming on PubMed before final inclusion.
